Have a PDF that OpenAlex does not index (a preprint, a book chapter, an internal report)? Add it by its path on this machine and it joins the pipeline at extraction, no harvest needed. The DOI and title are read from the PDF when you leave them blank.

After adding, run Extract (Pipeline tab) to process it, or Run pipeline with “skip harvest” checked.

About alpminer

Turn a body of literature into a structured recipe table

ALPminer searches the scientific literature, collects the PDFs, and uses an AI model to read each paper and pull out its process recipes (precursors, temperatures, timings, growth per cycle, and the other fields you care about) into one clean table you can export. You drive the whole thing from the tabs above; this page explains how.

Before you start · one-time setup
  • Create the project. The first time you open this folder, enter your email and click Create project. The email just identifies polite traffic to the free OpenAlex / Unpaywall services and is never used for anything else.
  • Choose your AI provider. In Settings, pick a provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or a local / open-source server) and paste its API key. Keys are held for this session only and are never written to disk; set them again next time, or store them in your system environment so they load automatically.
  • Optional: tune what you search and extract. Still in Settings, the Extraction rules card lets you edit the search query and the exact list of fields the AI pulls out (add, rename, or remove any of them), or switch between the built-in ALD and ALE profiles. To build your own, pick ＋ Create new profile… in the profile dropdown: name it, then start from a blank template or a copy of any existing profile. (Editing rules and clicking Save as new profile… does the same from the editor.) Custom profiles you no longer need can be deleted from the same dropdown; the built-ins can never be modified or deleted.
How the pipeline works · four steps

01Harvest metadata

Pipeline → Fetch metadata. Searches OpenAlex for every article matching your query and stores its title, authors, journal, and DOI, just the metadata, no PDFs yet. It is resumable, so a big harvest can be stopped and continued anytime.

02Get the PDFs

Two ways, and you can use both. Download PDFs (Pipeline tab) automatically fetches the legal open-access copies. Everything without a free copy waits in the Manual queue: open the DOI links, download each through your library, and drop the files into the inbox folder, where they match to their paper automatically by the DOI or title on the page, whatever the filename. Have a PDF that is not in OpenAlex (a preprint, a report)? Add a paper not in OpenAlex on that same tab drops it straight into the pipeline. And a paper you don't want? Remove takes it out of the queue; it waits in the Removed papers list, restorable anytime.

03Extract the recipes

Pipeline → Extract recipes. The AI first triages each paper (is it even relevant?) with a cheap model, then reads the full text of the ones that pass and fills in your fields. Every response is cached, so re-running never pays for the same paper twice, and failed papers retry automatically next time. Big run? Raise Parallel LLM calls in Settings to extract several papers at once. Scanned PDFs with no text layer can be OCR'd, either as they appear or deferred to the end of the run (Settings → OCR timing).

04Review and export

Browse everything on the Recipes tab: search it, and click any row to see the full record and where in the paper it came from. Export view (CSV) downloads exactly what your search matches; Rebuild exports (Dashboard) writes the full CSV / JSON dataset. Every exported record carries an ocr flag telling you whether its text came from the PDF itself or from OCR.

In a hurry? Run pipeline (Dashboard or Pipeline tab) does all four steps end to end. The Limit per stage box keeps a first trial small and cheap.

Good to know
  • Stopping is always safe. Every paper is saved the moment it finishes, so you can hit Stop job or close the app at any point and pick up exactly where you left off; nothing is lost or repaid.
  • Watch the cost. The est. tokens left gauge and the per-stage limits help you control spend; triage runs on a cheap model and only extraction uses the strong one. The Dashboard also shows the actual tokens spent so far, straight from the provider's own usage reports. Google Gemini has a free tier if you are just trying things out.
  • Missing growth-per-cycle is filled in for you. When a paper gives a film thickness and a cycle count but no GPC, alpminer computes it (thickness ÷ cycles) and marks the value auto-derived so you always know which numbers were measured and which were calculated.
  • The fields are yours to change. Edit them anytime in Settings → Extraction rules. Because one project stores one field set, switching profiles or changing fields is cleanest after a Danger zone → Reset.
  • Auto-file PDFs. On the Manual queue tab you can have alpminer watch your inbox (and even your Downloads folder) and file PDFs the instant they arrive, copying them in without touching your originals.
  • Scanned (image-only) PDFs are recoverable. Install OCR once (pip install "alpminer[ocr]" plus Tesseract) and turn it on in Settings. Choose inline to OCR each scan as it is reached, or deferred to finish all normal papers first and OCR the flagged ones at the end of the same run. OCR-sourced records are always marked (ocr column / badge) since OCR can misread characters.
